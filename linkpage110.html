Day 2: Farasan Island

Drive to Farasan Island (59km)

Head to the magical boat trip around the virgin islands of Farasan to enjoy swimming, snorkeling, fishing.

Enjoy lunch meal at one of the virgin islands.

 

End the day with a sunset view on the island then head to the hotel for a shower.

Head on a historical trip to visit Al-Qessar Village, A top attraction on the island is Al Qassar Village. This little village hosts the largest palm oasis in Farasan, as it was once an ancient summer resort. Stones and mud structures dominate the area giving you a taste of the sand colored world residents would have once known. 

Then move to visit Al-Najdi Mosque, The Al Najdi mosque was constructed by Ibrahim Al Najdi, who was one of the biggest pearl merchants on the island. Surrounded by houses on all sides, the mosque sits inconspicuously in a densely packed neighborhood.

 

The walls of the mosque are decorated with plaster-recessed ribs and each window on the qibla wall is topped with gypsum-decorated arches. The wooden mimbar and mihrab are said to be brought from India and are adorned with colorful decorative ornaments.

Overnight in Jazan.

Day 3 : Al Qandal Beach – Mangrove Forests

Drive to Al-Qandal Beach, The Qandal Forest is filled with mangroves trees, which are interspersed with waterways and bays.

 

The Qandal Forest is located in the north of the island of Forsan.
Then move to visit Mangrove Forests. This location is situated in the main Farasan island which boasts breathtaking sea views and mangrove forests, with waterways cutting through them. It is located some 50km offshore from Jizan, the far southwestern part of Saudi Arabia. It is surrounded by mangrove forest, crystal-clear water, and beautiful coral reefs. It is around 30 km far away from the ferry terminal of the Farsan island which boasts breathtaking sea views and coral reef mountain.
